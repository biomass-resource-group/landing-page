The Kenya operation has completed the core staffing and field-readiness work required for launch. The team structure now covers operations management, field engineering, logistics coordination, and commercial relationships needed to move from preparation into active deployment.

That staffing milestone matters because BRG's East Africa model depends on disciplined execution in distributed environments. Mobile kilns only work economically when sourcing, transport, site operations, and downstream buyer coordination are handled as one integrated system.

With personnel and local relationships in place, the next phase is deployment sequencing tied to financing and equipment mobilization. The operation is positioned to convert invasive Prosopis feedstock into biochar while building the monitoring trail required for verified carbon removal issuance.

## Readiness priorities now in place

- In-country operating leadership and field engineering coverage
- Logistics pathways for feedstock movement and deployment planning
- Buyer-side coordination for biochar offtake and carbon-market engagement

The Kenya platform remains a foundational proof point for BRG's replication strategy across emerging markets.
