Biomass Resource Group has formally established the holding-company structure intended to unify its operating businesses under a single strategic platform. The change is designed to make BRG easier to understand from the outside and easier to scale from the inside.

Instead of presenting each project as a standalone effort, the holding-company model clarifies how shared logistics, carbon monitoring, buyer development, and capital formation work across multiple regions. That is the underlying operating reality of the business, and the new structure makes that reality explicit.

For partners and investors, the result is a cleaner entry point into the company. Governance, capital allocation, and regional expansion now sit inside a framework designed for a portfolio of operating entities rather than a collection of disconnected pilots.

## What changes with the new structure

- Corporate oversight is centralized while regional execution remains local.
- Shared capabilities can be reused across East Africa, Saudi Arabia, and the active pipeline.
- Future solution lines can be added without rebuilding the platform architecture each time.

The website and communications stack are being updated to reflect the same platform-level framing.
