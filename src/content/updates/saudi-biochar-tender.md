The Saudi platform has advanced into the final round of evaluation for a large national water company biogas tender tied to sludge-processing infrastructure. The opportunity matters because it combines contracted waste-processing revenue with long-duration operating visibility and a direct pathway into higher-throughput biochar production.

For BRG, the tender is more than a single contract. It is a validation point for the operating thesis behind the Saudi business: integrate with existing waste and water infrastructure, co-locate processing capacity with dependable feedstock, and build recurring revenue that does not depend exclusively on carbon prices.

The final-round work is focused on engineering scope, delivery sequencing, and the commercial structure needed for long-term operation. If awarded, the contract would become the most substantial government-linked infrastructure relationship in the BRG portfolio to date.

## Why it matters

- It deepens the procurement channel that underpins BRG's industrial model in Saudi Arabia.
- It strengthens the case for fixed-kiln deployment tied to stable municipal feedstock streams.
- It creates another route for combining waste processing, biogas recovery, and carbon removal in one operating system.

Further updates will follow once the tender process moves from finalist review to award decision.
